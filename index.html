Welcome to TMAI Group!

Medical-Engineering Integration/Intelligent Medical Engineering and Health Informatics

The Trustworthy Medical AI (TMAI) group, relying on The Key Laboratory of Brain Health Intelligent Evaluation and Intervention of the Ministry of Education, our group focuses on privacy-preserving AI healthcare applications. Our goal is to develop trustworthy AI methods and models that advance precision, data-driven healthcare while safeguarding sensitive medical data.

The current research focuses on the early screening and diagnosis of brain-cardiovascular health.

Research Directions

  1. Trustworthy Medical AI Modeling

    We aim to address reliability, robustness, and interpretability challenges in real-world deployment of medical AI systems. Our work builds trustworthy AI models—such as robust federated learning frameworks—that ensure stable and reliable decision-making with complex, heterogeneous medical data.

  2. Privacy-Preserving LLMs/LMMs in Healthcare

    We investigate secure development and deployment of LLMs and multimodal foundation models for medical imaging, clinical text, physiological sensing, and other healthcare data. Our focus is on enabling advanced understanding and reasoning over both structured and unstructured clinical data while rigorously protecting patient privacy using privacy-enhancing technologies such as federated learning.

  3. Privacy-Preserving Psychophysiological Computing

    We explore AI methods that integrate psychophysiological sensing—e.g., emotion and stress indicators—with privacy-preserving computation. Techniques including federated learning, differential privacy, and homomorphic encryption are applied to protect highly sensitive mental and physiological data during acquisition, analysis, and modeling.
